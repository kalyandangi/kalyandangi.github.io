🚖 NYC Taxi Dashboard

This section explores key trends from the NYC Yellow, Green, and For-Hire Vehicle (FHV) taxi datasets for January 2015. I analyze demand across boroughs, fare structures, peak hours, and most traveled zones, helping us understand commuter behavior and urban mobility patterns.

🎥 Demo Walkthrough

💡 Key Insights

• Manhattan had the highest yellow taxi usage, accounting for over 92% of total trips.
• Midtown Center was the top pickup location for FHVs.
• Friday saw the highest taxi and flight activity, indicating weekly travel peaks.
• The average fare for green taxis was $11.77 and for yellow taxis around $82.53 in some high-fare zones.
• Weather conditions significantly impacted trip durations and delays, with colder days showing reduced mobility.

🗺️ Borough-Level Analysis

Manhattan leads in taxi usage. FHV pickups are high in Midtown Center. Fare disparities are evident, with Charleston/Tootenville topping average fares. Staten Island sees minimal taxi activity.

📆 Weekday vs Weekend Behavior

Friday is the busiest day. Mornings have higher fares. FHV demand rises on weekends. Weekend travel is more balanced among boroughs, while weekdays skew towards business districts.

🌡️ Weather Impact

January’s temperatures dropped significantly. Low temps and high variance between actual and “feels like” values reduced taxi demand and increased flight delays. 2 AM shows the highest variance.

✈️ Flight Delay & Cancellation Trends

Most delays on Mondays; Fridays have the fewest. Southwest leads delays. Dallas/Fort Worth leads cancellations. Delays and cancellations diverge sharply in January.

🧪 Hypothesis Testing

• Delays rise in poor weather
• Weekend fares slightly higher
• Arrival & departure delays are positively correlated
Explored using filters across airlines, dates, and weather conditions in Power BI.
