🚖 NYC Taxi Dashboard

This section explores key trends from the NYC Yellow, Green, and For-Hire Vehicle (FHV) taxi datasets for January 2015. I analyze demand across boroughs, fare structures, peak hours, and most traveled zones, helping us understand commuter behavior and urban mobility patterns.

🎥 Demo Walkthrough

💡 Key Insights

• Manhattan had the highest yellow taxi usage, accounting for over 92% of total trips.
• Midtown Center was the top pickup location for FHVs.
• Friday saw the highest taxi and flight activity, indicating weekly travel peaks.
• The average fare for green taxis was $11.77 and for yellow taxis around $82.53 in some high-fare zones.
• Weather conditions significantly impacted trip durations and delays, with colder days showing reduced mobility.

🗺️ Borough-Level Analysis

This section focuses on how taxi demand and fare patterns vary across NYC boroughs. Manhattan consistently leads in taxi usage, with over 92% of yellow taxi trips. In contrast, Staten Island sees minimal demand. For-Hire Vehicle (FHV) data shows Midtown Center as the most active pickup zone. The analysis also reveals fare disparities, with neighborhoods like Charleston/Tootenville showing the highest average fares.

📆 Weekday vs Weekend Behavior

Travel behavior shifts significantly between weekdays and weekends. Data shows Friday as the busiest day for both flights and taxis, with weekday mornings seeing higher average fares. Yellow taxi demand peaks early mornings, while FHV demand increases on weekends. Week 5’s Friday alone accounts for 6.32% of green taxi demand. Weekend activity is more evenly distributed among boroughs.

🌡️ Weather Impact

Weather conditions had a measurable impact on NYC's mobility. As temperatures dropped from Jan 1 to Jan 31, both minimum and maximum temperatures showed steep declines. Temperature variance between “feels like” and actual temperatures was highest around 2 AM. The coldest days saw reduced taxi demand and increased flight delays, suggesting weather was a significant disruptor.

✈️ Flight Delay & Cancellation Trends

The analysis revealed that flight delays and cancellations peaked on Mondays, while Fridays had the fewest. Southwest Airlines had the highest number of delays (42,195), and Dallas/Fort Worth Airport experienced the most cancellations (3,578). Delay patterns align with weather and weekday behavior, while cancellations diverged most during January. A noticeable gap between delays and cancellations highlights complex operational dynamics in NYC airports.

🧪 Hypothesis Testing

Several hypotheses were tested to better understand correlations. For instance, we found:

  • Flight delays increase when weather is "snow" or "rain."
  • Taxi fares are marginally higher on weekends, especially during peak evening hours.
  • Arrival and departure delays are positively correlated, indicating cascading schedule effects.
These insights were explored using filters across airline, weather condition, and date dimensions in Power BI.

📊 Anomaly Detection

This section highlights unusual values and outliers:

  • 🚖 Max fare: $4,008 (Yellow Taxi)
  • 🕒 Max trip duration: 549,000 minutes
  • 🌡️ Coldest temperature: -16.5°C | Feels like: -27.1°C
  • 🛫 Min flight distance: 31 miles | Min arrival delay: -82 mins
Anomaly flags helped uncover errors, extreme use cases, and data quality concerns, particularly in FHV datasets.

🔗 How It’s Connected

This project brings together three distinct datasets: taxi trips, weather, and flight operations. Relationships were established via date, location IDs, and weather conditions to allow cross-domain analysis. For example:

  • Taxi pickup dates align with weather data to assess weather impacts on demand.
  • Flight delays are analyzed against airport origin and daily weather.
  • FHV trip data includes weather joins for contextual analysis despite missing drop-off details.
These connections enable a rich, narrative-driven exploration of how transportation systems interact with environmental and temporal factors in a major urban setting.
