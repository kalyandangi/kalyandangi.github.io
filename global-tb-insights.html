üìå Project Overview

This interactive dashboard explores the global burden of tuberculosis (TB) between 1990 and 2015. Built using Power BI, the dashboard presents a visual and analytical breakdown of TB's prevalence, incidence, and mortality across different world regions and countries.

üß† Key Insights & Findings

Global Totals (1990‚Äì2013):
  • Total Population: 149 Billion
  • TB Prevalence: 340.7 Million
  • TB Deaths: 44.35 Million
  • Incident Cases: 216 Million
  • India consistently leads in TB prevalence and TB-related deaths.
  • About 20.76% of TB deaths are HIV-related.
  • Average TB detection rate is highest in Europe (79%), and lowest in Africa (51%).
  • In 2013 alone: 7B population, 11.4M TB prevalence, 1.43M deaths, 8.96M incidents.

üìä Dashboard Pages

1. Global Overview

  • Cards showing total prevalence, deaths, incidents
  • Donut chart of TB deaths by HIV status
  • Map with hover-over country-level death data
  • Year/country/region slicers for dynamic filtering

2. TB Prevalence

  • Funnel chart: 92.97% of prevalence data is predicted
  • Line chart compares high vs low bounds
  • Pie chart: AFR = 43% of global prevalence

3. TB Incidence

  • South Africa leads with 4M HIV-positive TB cases
  • Top incidence methods: High income estimation, mortality estimation
  • 89.71% non-HIV TB; 10.29% HIV-positive

4. TB Death

  • Peak TB deaths in 2001: 2.1M
  • Majority from non-HIV TB (63.04%)
  • Swaziland and Zimbabwe: highest HIV in TB incident rates

5. Summary Page

  • Max case detection rate in 1995 (320%)
  • TB Prevalence: 0.23% | Incidents: 0.14% | Deaths: 0.03%
  • TB metrics by country per 100k population

üåç Observations

  • TB burden is highest in low-income and HIV-affected countries.
  • Strong regional disparities in detection and mortality rates.
  • Steady decline in TB deaths post-2001 due to interventions.
